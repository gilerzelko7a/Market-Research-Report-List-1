<p><p>The alcohol-based disinfectant market comprises various types of alcohols, each with distinct properties and applications. Ethyl alcohol (ethanol) is widely used in hand sanitizers and surface disinfectants due to its effectiveness against pathogens. Isopropyl alcohol (isopropanol) is commonly employed in medical and household disinfectants for its rapid evaporation and efficacy. n-Propyl alcohol is less prevalent but is used in specialized applications. Methyl alcohol (methanol) is primarily avoided in disinfectants due to toxicity concerns, limiting its market presence.</p></p>
