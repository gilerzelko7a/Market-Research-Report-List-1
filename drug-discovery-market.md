<p><p>The drug discovery market can be categorized into two primary types: small molecule drugs and biologics. Small molecule drugs are typically low molecular weight compounds that can easily penetrate cells, often used for conditions like cancer and cardiovascular diseases. Biologics, on the other hand, are complex molecules derived from living organisms, including proteins, vaccines, and monoclonal antibodies, utilized for various diseases, particularly chronic and autoimmune disorders. Both markets are critical for advancing therapeutic options and addressing unmet medical needs.</p></p>
