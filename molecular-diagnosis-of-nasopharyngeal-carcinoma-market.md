<p><p>Molecular diagnosis of nasopharyngeal carcinoma involves various techniques, including PCR, FISH, DNA sequencing, and gene chips. PCR amplifies specific DNA sequences to detect genetic alterations. FISH uses fluorescent probes to identify chromosomal abnormalities. DNA sequencing provides a detailed genetic profile, revealing mutations linked to the cancer. Gene chips enable simultaneous analysis of thousands of genes, offering a comprehensive view of gene expression changes. Together, these methods enhance early detection, prognosis, and personalized treatment strategies in managing nasopharyngeal carcinoma.</p></p>
