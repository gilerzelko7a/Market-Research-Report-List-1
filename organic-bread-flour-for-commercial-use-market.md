<p><p>Organic bread flour for commercial use is typically produced through two main milling methods: machine milled flour and stone ground flour. Machine milled flour is crafted using industrial equipment that processes large batches quickly, resulting in a fine, consistent texture suitable for high-volume baking. In contrast, stone ground flour is made by grinding grains between traditional stones, preserving more nutrients and flavor, which appeals to artisanal bakers. Both types cater to different market demands, from mass production to specialty baking.</p></p>
