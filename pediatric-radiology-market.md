<p><p>The Pediatric Radiology market encompasses various imaging modalities tailored for children, including ultrasound, X-ray, magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), nuclear medicine, and computed tomography (CT). Ultrasound utilizes sound waves for real-time imaging, ideal for soft tissues. X-ray provides quick assessments of bone structures. MRI uses magnetic fields for detailed soft tissue images, while nuclear medicine involves radioactive tracers to visualize organ function. CT combines X-ray technology for cross-sectional imaging, offering detailed insights into complex conditions. Each modality serves specific diagnostic needs in pediatric care.</p></p>
